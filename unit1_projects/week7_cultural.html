Values

I've always chased knowledge. I like to know as much as possible about everything. Sometimes, this can be a detriment as I spend too much time reading and not enough time doing. As I have gotten older and gained more responsibilities (wife and kids), I have gotten much better at striking the right balance.

I'm not sure why knowledge is so important to me, but I have always loved to know how things work. I take pride in learning new ways to cook for my family, teach my son, and provide my family with a better life.

Recent Advice

As a high school teacher, I have had the privilege of advising teenagers on their future. Unfortunately, our society tells young people that they need to go to college or they are losers. The reality is many blue collar workers make as much or more than college graduates without going to college and losing 4-5 years of wages while building tens of thousands in student loan debt. I believe people should follow their passion. If you like working with your hands then become a carpenter, but if you want to be a doctor then college is the right choice for you.
